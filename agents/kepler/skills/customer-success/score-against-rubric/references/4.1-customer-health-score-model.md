**Customer Health Score**

CSM Standard Operating Procedure

*How scores are calculated and what you need to keep updated in Salesforce*

# Overview

The Customer Health Score gives every CSM a real-time view of account health across five pillars, ordered from most to least actionable.

| **Segment** | **Engagement** | **Risk Flags** | **Support** | **Product Usage** | **Financial** | **Special Notes** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **SMB (CX)** | **20%** | **20%** | **20%** | **30%** | **25%** | Standard CRM — SMB. Usage weighted highest at 30%. |
| **MM (CX)** | **20%** | **20%** | **20%** | **20%** | **20%** | Standard CRM — Mid-Market. Equal 20% weights across all pillars. |
| **Enterprise (CX)** | **20%** | **20%** | **15%** | **20%** | **25%** | Standard CRM — Enterprise. Financial weighted at 25%. Support reduced to 15%. |
| **Alleva (pre 2025)** | **10%** | **40%** | **0%** | **50%** | **0%** | Support and Financial removed — Alleva pays in full upfront and has no Intercom access. Risk (40%) and Usage (50%) are primary signals. Engagement (10%) uses modified formula: (Cadence + Sentiment + 0.50) ÷ 3. |
| **Alleva (post 2025)** | **5%** | **50%** | **0%** | **30%** | **0%** | Support and Financial removed — same reasoning as pre 2025. Risk (30%) and Usage (50%) are primary signals. Engagement (20%) uses (Cadence + Sentiment) ÷ 2. Full 0–100 Engagement range. |
| **Implementation** | **0%** | **30%** | **0%** | **0%** | **0%** | Temporary segment for accounts not yet live. Only Risk Flag (30%) and Implementation Status (70%) scored. Update segment at handoff to CSM. |

*Each pillar scores 0.01–1.00. The Master Health Score (MHS\_Calc\_\_c) is weighted by segment and displayed as 0–100.*

# Pillar 1 — Customer Engagement Score

The pillar most in your control. Three sub-scores averaged together.

**API Name: Customer\_Engagement\_avgScore\_\_c**

## Sub-Score 1: Meeting Cadence Score (Meeting\_Cadence\_Score\_\_c)

**Field you manage: Meeting Cadence (picklist on Account)**

| **Meeting Cadence Value** | **Score** |
| --- | --- |
| 2x Weekly | 1.00 |
| Weekly | 1.00 |
| 2x Monthly | 0.90 |
| Monthly | 0.85 |
| Every 2 Months | 0.75 |
| Quarterly | 0.70 |
| Engaged - Ad Hoc | 0.60 |
| Not Engaged | 0.10 |

**What to do:**

* Keep Meeting Cadence updated whenever your cadence changes.
* Weekly or 2x Weekly is the target — anything below Monthly will drag the score.

## Sub-Score 2: Sentiment Score (Sentiment\_Score\_\_c)

**Field you manage: Customer Sentiment (picklist on Account)**

| **Customer Sentiment Value** | **Score** |
| --- | --- |
| Positive | 1.00 |
| Neutral | 0.70 |
| Negative | 0.10 |
| Unknown | 0.01 |

**What to do:**

* Update Customer Sentiment after every meaningful interaction.
* Never leave it as Unknown — even Neutral is better than no data.
* Negative sentiment should always be paired with a Risk Flag.

## Sub-Score 3: EB Interaction Score (EB\_Interaction\_Score\_\_c)

*This score degrades automatically over time. Log EB interactions regularly to keep it high.*

**Auto-stamped field: Last\_Engagement\_with\_Economic\_Buyer\_\_c — populated by Flow when Event Type = CX Economic Buyer Meeting**

| **Days Since Last EB Interaction** | **Score** |
| --- | --- |
| No date on file | 0.01 |
| Less than 30 days | 1.00 |
| 30–59 days | 0.85 |
| 60–89 days | 0.75 |
| 90+ days | 0.01 |

**What to do:**

* Log every EB interaction as an Event with Type = CX Economic Buyer Meeting.
* Salesforce stamps the date automatically — no manual update needed.
* Goal is quarterly minimum. Score degrades automatically without logged interactions.

## Customer Engagement Score — Formula

**(Meeting Cadence Score + Sentiment Score + EB Interaction Score) / 3**

# Pillar 2 — Risk Flag Score

The second most actionable pillar. You own the Risk Flag lifecycle from creation to resolution.

**API Name: Risk\_Score\_\_c**

| **Condition** | **Score** | **What It Means** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Active Risk Flags > 0 | 0.10 | Critical — one or more unresolved risk flags |
| No Active, but Recovering > 0 | 0.75 | Watch — previously flagged, now recovering |
| No Active or Recovering flags | 1.00 | Healthy — no open risk flags |

| **Roll-Up Field** | **What It Counts** |
| --- | --- |
| Active\_Risk\_Flags\_\_c | Risk Flag records where Status = Active |
| Recovering\_Risk\_Flags\_\_c | Risk Flag records where Status = Recovering |
| Resolved\_Risk\_Flags\_\_c | Risk Flag records where Status = Resolved (reference only) |

**What to do:**

* Log Risk Flags promptly — the score updates automatically.
* Move flags: Active → Recovering as situation improves → Resolved when fully mitigated.
* A score of 0.10 should always have an active escalation plan.

# Pillar 3 — Support Score

You can't resolve tickets directly, but you can influence the customer's support experience. Data sourced from Intercom on a rolling 90-day basis.

**API Name: Support\_Score\_\_c**

| **Condition** | **Score** | **What It Means** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 6+ open tickets | 0.10 | Critical — escalate immediately |
| 3–5 open tickets | 0.50 | Moderate — proactive conversation needed |
| 1–2 open tickets | 0.85 | Minor — not a concern yet |
| 0 open, 3+ closed (90 day) | 1.00 | Healthy — issues getting resolved |
| 0 open, 1–2 closed (90 day) | 0.85 | Low but clean — light support usage |
| 0 open, 0 closed (90 day) | 0.65 | Mild flag — no support engagement |

| **Source Field** | **API Name** | **What It Represents** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Intercom Open Tickets | Intercom\_Open\_Tickets\_\_c | Currently open/unresolved tickets |
| Intercom Closed Tickets | Intercom\_Closed\_Tickets\_\_c | Tickets closed in last 90 days |
| Intercom Ticket Count | Intercom\_Ticket\_Count\_\_c | Total tickets in last 90 days |

**What to do:**

* 0 open + 0 closed scores 0.65 — a mild flag, not a crisis. Common for Hypercare and early adoption customers.
* 1–2 open tickets = 0.85 — these are engaged customers, not unhappy ones.
* 3–5 open tickets = 0.50 — reach out proactively to understand what's happening.
* 6+ open tickets = 0.10 — escalate to your Support lead immediately.
* Encourage customers to use support — resolved tickets are a healthy signal.

# Pillar 4 — Product Usage Score

You can coach customers toward better adoption but can't directly control usage. Data updates automatically via integration.

**API Name: Product\_Usage\_Score\_\_c**

## How It Works

Three usage ratios compare recent activity to prior periods. A ratio > 1.00 = growth. New customers with no prior period default to 1.00 — not penalized for being new.

| **Window** | **Ratio** | **Ratio Field** | **Score Field** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 Day | Yesterday / Day Before | Opp\_Usage\_Ratio\_1\_day\_\_c | Opp\_Usage\_Ratio\_1\_day\_Score\_\_c |
| 7 Day | Last 7 / Previous 7 | Opp\_Usage\_Ratio\_7\_Days\_\_c | Opp\_Usage\_Ratio\_7\_Days\_Score\_\_c |
| 6 Month | Last 6 Mo / Previous 6 Mo | Opp\_to\_Admin\_Usage\_Ratio\_6\_Month\_\_c | Opp\_to\_Admin\_Ratio\_6\_Month\_Score\_\_c |

| **Usage Ratio** | **Score** | **What It Means** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0 (no usage) | 0.01 | Critical — no activity detected |
| Less than 0.50 | 0.10 | Declining significantly |
| 0.50 – 0.74 | 0.50 | Declining moderately |
| 0.75 – 0.84 | 0.75 | Roughly flat |
| 0.85 – 0.99 | 0.85 | Stable or growing |
| 1.00+ (or new customer) | 1.00 | Growing or new — ideal |

## Product Usage Score — Formula

**(Opp Usage Ratio 1 Day Score + Opp Usage Ratio 7 Days Score + Opp to Admin Ratio 6 Month Score) / 3**

**What to do:**

* No manual action needed — data updates automatically.
* A low 1-day score alone is not alarming — check 7-day and 6-month for context.
* A ratio of 0 on any window means zero usage — investigate immediately.
* New customers show 1.00 for windows with no prior data — this is expected.

# Pillar 5 — Financial Score

Largely outside your control. If this score is low, flag it and escalate — don't try to resolve it yourself.

**API Name: Financial\_Score\_\_c**

| **Outstanding Balance as % of ARR** | **Score** | **What It Means** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0% (no outstanding balance) | 1.00 | Healthy — fully current on payments |
| 1% – 10% | 0.75 | Minor — small balance outstanding |
| 11% – 25% | 0.50 | Moderate — meaningful balance overdue |
| 26% – 50% | 0.25 | Serious — significant portion overdue |
| Greater than 50% | 0.01 | Critical — majority of ARR outstanding |
| ARR = 0, balance exists | 0.01 | Critical — no ARR on file but balance outstanding |

**What to do:**

* No manual input required — fields are populated automatically.
* If Financial Score is below 0.75, notify Finance or Collections.
* A score of 0.01 is a critical signal — escalate immediately.
* Weighted at 25% for SMB and Enterprise. 20% for MM (CX), Billing CRM, and iVerify only. 25% for IQ only. 0% for Implementation, Alleva (pre 2025), Alleva (post 2025), and Consultant segments.

# Implementation Phase

Temporary segment for accounts not yet live. Only Risk Flag (30%) and Implementation Status (70%) scored. Update segment at handoff to CSM.

**API Name:**

| **Status** | **Score** | **What It Means** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| On Track | 1.00 | Healthy |
| Not Started | 0.75 | Not started due to just closing the deal, not delayed |
| Delayed | 0.50 | Behind, but customer is OK, maybe a little upset. If customer is very upset there should be a Risk Flag |
| Blocked | 0.25 | Serious — Risk Flag should be filed. Both parties are working towards a resolution. Stopped due to technical/operational dependency, team actively working to resolve |
| Unresponsive Customer | 0.10 | Customer has gone dark, not responding |
| On Hold - Payment Due | 0.01 | Work deliberately paused by [Company] pending payment |
| Complete | 1.00 | Done! |

**What to know:**

* Risk ×30% + Implementation Status ×70% = Score

# Master Health Score (MHS)

A single number between 0 and 100 calculated automatically by a Salesforce Flow (Calculate Master Health Score) whenever an Account is saved.

## Salesforce Field Details

| **Field** | **API Name** | **Type** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Master Health Score | MHS\_Calc\_\_c | Number — stamped by Flow |
| CX Health Score Segment | CX\_Health\_Score\_Segment\_\_c | Picklist |
| Health Score Indicator | Health\_Score\_Indicator\_\_c | Formula (Text) |

**Segment picklist values: SMB (CX) | MM (CX) | Enterprise (CX) | Alleva (pre 2025) | Alleva (post 2025) | iVerify only | IQ only | Billing CRM | Implementation | Consultant. If blank, defaults to MM (CX).**

## Formula by Segment

| **Segment** | **Formula** |
| --- | --- |
| SMB (CX) | (Engagement x 5%) + (Risk x 20%) + (Support x 20%) + (Usage x 30%) + (Financial x 25%) |
| MM (CX) | (Engagement x 20%) + (Risk x 20%) + (Support x 20%) + (Usage x 20%) + (Financial x 20%) |
| Enterprise (CX) | (Engagement x 20%) + (Risk x 20%) + (Support x 15%) + (Usage x 20%) + (Financial x 25%) |
| Alleva (pre 2025) | ((Cadence + Sentiment + 0.50) / 3 x 10%) + (Risk x 40%) + (Usage x 50%). Support and Financial = 0%. EB hardcoded to 0.50 — no EB relationship. Max Engagement = 0.83. |
| Alleva (post 2025) | ((Cadence + Sentiment) / 2 x 20%) + (Risk x 30%) + (Usage x 50%). Support and Financial = 0%. EB removed — full 0–100 Engagement range. |
| *iVerify only* | *(Engagement x 20%) + (Risk x 20%) + (Support x 20%) + (Financial x 20%) + (Product Usage x 20%). Product Usage = VOB-based (New Instant VOBs Last 30 Days). Thresholds: 10+ = 1.00, 5–9 = 0.75, 1–4 = 0.50, 0 = 0.10.* |
| *IQ only* | *(Engagement x 25%) + (Risk x 25%) + (Support x 25%) + (Financial x 25%). Product Usage Score = 0% — no usage data available for IQ-only customers. Remaining four pillars split equally at 25%.* |
| *Billing CRM* | *(Engagement x 20%) + (Risk x 20%) + (Support x 20%) + (Financial x 20%) + (Product Usage x 20%). Product Usage = VOB-based (New Instant VOBs Last 30 Days). Thresholds: 50+ = 1.00, 20–49 = 0.75, 1–19 = 0.50, 0 = 0.10.* |
| *Implementation* | *(Risk × 30%) + (Implementation Status ×70%)* |

**Key things to know:**

* Flow fires on every Account save and stamps MHS\_Calc\_\_c automatically.
* SMB weights Product Usage at 30% — usage is the strongest signal at this scale.
* Customer Engagement is only 5% for SMB — usage is a stronger proxy than meeting cadence.
* MM (CX) has equal weighting (20% each) — no single pillar dominates.
* Enterprise weights Financial at 25% — financial standing is critical for larger contracts.
* Alleva (pre 2025) uses modified weights: Engagement 10%, Risk 40%, Usage 50%. Support and Financial are both 0% — Alleva has no Intercom access and pays upfront in full. EB Interaction is hardcoded to 0.50. Max Engagement Score = 0.83.
* Alleva (post 2025) uses modified weights: Engagement 20%, Risk 30%, Usage 50%. Support and Financial are both 0% — same reasoning as pre 2025. EB removed from Engagement formula — averaged across Cadence and Sentiment only (÷ 2). Full 0–100 Engagement range.
* Implementation, Billing CRM, iVerify only, and IQ only are fully built with dedicated scoring logic. See the New Segments section below and the segment weights table for full details.

Implementation segment scores only Risk Flag (50%) and Implementation Status (50%) — all other pillars are 0%. This is a temporary segment for accounts not yet live. Update CX Health Score Segment to the correct post-implementation segment (SMB, MM, Enterprise, etc.) immediately at handoff.

Billing CRM uses equal 20% weights across all five pillars. Product Usage is VOB-based (New Instant VOBs Last 30 Days) — 50+ = 1.00, 20–49 = 0.75, 1–19 = 0.50, 0 = 0.10.

iVerify only uses equal 20% weights across all five pillars. Product Usage is VOB-based with lower thresholds — 10+ = 1.00, 5–9 = 0.75, 1–4 = 0.50, 0 = 0.10.

IQ only weights Product Usage at 0% — no usage data is available for IQ-only customers. The remaining four pillars split equally at 25% each.

## Score Interpretation

| **Master Health Score** | **Indicator** | **Recommended Action** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 80 – 100 | Green | Healthy — maintain cadence, look for expansion |
| 65 – 79 | Yellow | Needs attention — identify lagging pillar(s) |
| Below 65 | Red | At risk — escalate, create remediation plan |

# Frequently Asked Questions

## Why is my EB Interaction Score 0.01?

Either no EB interaction has been logged, or it has been 90+ days. Log a new CX Economic Buyer Meeting Event and the score updates automatically.

## Why is my Sentiment Score 0.01?

Customer Sentiment is set to Unknown or blank. Update it to Positive, Neutral, or Negative.

## Do I need to manually update the Last Engagement with Economic Buyer date?

No. Log the Event with Type = CX Economic Buyer Meeting and Salesforce stamps the date automatically.

## My customer has zero support tickets — why is Support Score 0.65 and not 1.00?

Zero tickets can mean the customer is disengaged or not yet encountering issues. It is a mild flag (0.65), not a crisis. Hypercare customers and those in early adoption often show 0.65 — use your judgment and look at other pillars for context.

## Why is my Product Usage Score low even though the customer seems active?

The score compares recent to prior periods. A low 1-day score is common — check the 7-day and 6-month scores for the full picture. If data looks wrong, contact RevOps.

## Why is Master Health Score not updating?

MHS Calc is stamped by the Calculate Master Health Score Flow when an Account is saved. Try editing and saving the Account. If still not updating, check the Flow is Active in Setup.

## What does the Health Score Indicator mean?

Green = MHS Calc >= 80. Yellow = 65–79. Red = below 65. Updates automatically whenever the Account is saved.

## What is the difference between the old Health Score and MHS Calc?

The old Health Score was manually entered. MHS Calc is fully automated, calculated from all five pillar scores weighted by segment. The old field will be retired once all data is validated.

## Why is Product Usage Score 0% for IQ only customers?

IQ only customers have no opportunity or VOB data available. Product Usage is zeroed out and the remaining four pillars (Engagement, Risk, Support, Financial) each carry 25% weight.

## Why do Billing CRM and iVerify only have different Product Usage scoring?

Billing CRM and iVerify only use New Instant VOBs Last 30 Days as their usage signal — not opportunity-based ratios. Thresholds differ: Billing CRM customers run higher volumes (50+ = 1.00) vs iVerify only (10+ = 1.00).

## Why does the Implementation segment need to be updated at handoff?

Update the CX Health Score Segment field on the Account to the correct post-implementation value (SMB, MM, Enterprise, etc.) immediately at the point of handoff. Leaving it as 'Implementation' after go-live will cause an incorrect health score. This is a required step in the CSM handoff checklist.

**SECTION 1 — OVERVIEW**

| **Purpose** | Ensure every CSM knows when, how, and why to file a Risk Flag in SFDC — and how to manage it through to resolution. Risk Flags are the primary driver of the Risk Flag Score pillar in the Master Health Score. |
| --- | --- |
| **Who This Is For** | All CSMs. Risk Flag hygiene is non-negotiable — the team is doing a great job filing flags. This SOP ensures consistency in how they are filed, managed, and resolved. |
| **Why It Matters** | A single Active Risk Flag drops the Risk Flag Score to 0.10 — the equivalent of a red account regardless of all other pillars. Filing promptly and resolving cleanly protects health scores and ensures leadership has an accurate picture of the book. |
| **The Golden Rule** | File it now. Do not wait for the risk to get worse, for your next call, or for someone else to flag it. If you know about a risk and it is not in SFDC, it does not exist. |
| **Escalation Path** | CSM → Anna → Sarah |
| **Key Rule** | All notes live in Chatter (SFDC) — NOT Slack. Every flag creation, status change, and resolution must be logged. |

**SECTION 2 — RISK FLAG STATUS DEFINITIONS & HEALTH SCORE IMPACT**

**ℹ** The Risk Flag Score updates automatically in real time as status changes. Moving a flag from Active to Recovering is not just administrative — it immediately improves the customer's health score.

| **Status** | **Health Score** | **What It Means** | **When to Use** | **Action Required** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Active** | **0.10** | A known risk exists and is unresolved. The account needs active intervention. | File immediately when risk is identified. Do not wait. | Active escalation plan required. Log in Chatter. |
| **Recovering** | **0.75** | Progress is being made but the risk is not fully resolved. Situation is improving. | Move from Active when concrete progress has been made. | Continue monitoring. Update Chatter with progress notes. |
| **Resolved** | **1.00** | Risk is fully mitigated. Customer confirmed. No further action needed. | Move from Recovering only when fully closed out. | Log resolution in Chatter. Close the flag. |

**SECTION 3 — HOW TO SUBMIT AND MANAGE A RISK FLAG (PART 1)**

**⚠** File immediately when a risk is identified. A Risk Flag filed today is a problem being managed. A Risk Flag filed next week is a problem that got worse.

| **#** | **Step** | **Action** | **Owner** | **Timing / Resources** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **1** | **Identify the risk** | As soon as you become aware of a risk — through a customer call, product usage data, a support ticket, a Slack message, or your own instinct — that is your trigger to file. Do not wait for the risk to get worse. Do not wait until your next call. File it now. | CSM | Immediately See Part 2 for risk type definitions |
| **2** | **Create Risk Flag record in SFDC** | Navigate to the Risk\_Flag\_\_c object in SFDC and create a new record. Required fields: - Account (link to the correct account) - Risk Type (select from the 12 types — see Part 2) - Status = Active - Description (brief but specific — what is the risk, when did you learn about it, what is the customer impact?) | CSM | Same day risk is identified SFDC → Risk\_Flag\_\_c → New Record Risk Type picklist — 12 types |
| **3** | **Log context in Chatter** | Immediately after creating the Risk Flag, log a Chatter note on the Account with: - What the risk is - When you learned about it - What the customer said or did that triggered the flag - What your initial plan is This is the record leadership will reference. Make it useful. | CSM | Same day as flag creation Account → Chatter feed |
| **4** | **Escalate if needed** | Some risk types require immediate escalation — do not try to handle alone. Escalate to Anna immediately for: Potential Churn Risk, Leadership Alignment, Financial Issue (significant), Lost Champion. See Part 2 for escalation guidance by risk type. | CSM → Anna | Same day for high-priority types Escalation path: CSM → Anna → Sarah |
| **5** | **Update status as situation evolves** | Move the flag from Active → Recovering when concrete progress has been made. Move from Recovering → Resolved when the risk is fully mitigated and the customer has confirmed. Never leave a flag stale. If it has been Active for more than 2 weeks with no update, that is a flag for your manager too. | CSM | Ongoing — update within weekly cadence Risk\_Flag\_\_c → Status field Chatter — log every status change with context |
| **6** | **Verify health score has updated** | After filing or resolving a flag, check the Master Health Score on the Account to confirm it has recalculated correctly. Active flag: score should show 0.10 for Risk Flag pillar. Recovering: should show 0.75. All resolved: should show 1.00. | CSM | After each status change Account → Master\_Health\_Score\_\_c SFDC: Account Health Overview Report |

**SECTION 4 — QUICK RULES**

**•** File immediately — never wait more than 24 hours after identifying a risk

**•** One flag per issue — do not file duplicate flags for the same root cause

**•** Be specific in the Description field — vague flags are not actionable

**•** Never leave a flag at Active for more than 2 weeks without a Chatter update

**•** Recovering means progress is real — do not move to Recovering just to improve the score

**•** Resolved means the customer has confirmed the issue is gone — not just that you think it is

**•** Some flag types require escalation — see Part 2 for type-specific guidance

**•** Potential Churn Risk flags must always trigger Anna immediately — no exceptions

**•** All notes in Chatter — not Slack

**✕** If you have an Active Risk Flag on an account that is coming up for renewal, it must have a documented Save Plan on the Renewal Opp before the renewal conversation begins. See the Renewal Playbook.

**SECTION 5 — RISK FLAG TYPE DEFINITIONS & RESOLUTION GUIDE (PART 2)**

**ℹ** All 12 risk flag types are defined below. Red rows = highest priority, escalate immediately. Amber rows = high priority, loop Anna in early. Use this as your daily reference when deciding which flag type to select.

| **Risk Type** | **File When** | **Trigger Signals** | **Resolution Path** | **Who to Involve** | **Escalate If** |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Integration Issue** | EHR, VoIP, billing, or webform integration is broken, degraded, or not working as expected. Customer is experiencing workflow disruption as a result. | *Customer reports errors, data not syncing, calls not logging, forms not submitting.* | CSM + Tech Services. Identify the root cause. Submit support/engineering ticket. Keep customer updated on timeline. Resolved when integration is confirmed working and customer has verified. | **CSM + Tech Services** | Anna if unresolved > 5 business days |
| **Financial Issue** | Outstanding balance exists, payment is late, billing dispute has been raised, or the Financial Score drops below 0.75. | *Financial Score < 0.75 in SFDC. Customer raises billing concern. Finance flags overdue balance.* | CSM loops in Finance/Collections immediately. Do not try to handle billing issues without Finance. Resolved when balance is cleared and Financial Score returns to 1.00. | **CSM + Finance / Collections** | Anna immediately for significant balances |
| **Bugs** | A confirmed product bug is affecting the customer's ability to use [Company] effectively. | *Customer reports unexpected behavior. Support ticket confirms it is a bug, not user error. Reproducible issue.* | CSM submits support ticket and tracks to resolution. Set clear expectations with the customer on timeline. Provide a workaround if available. Resolved when bug is fixed and customer confirms the issue is gone. | **CSM + Support + Engineering** | Anna if bug is blocking critical workflows |
| **Missing Features** | Customer needs a feature or capability that does not currently exist in [Company] and it is affecting their ability to get full value. | *Customer explicitly requests a feature. CSM identifies a workflow gap. Customer is using a workaround that is unsustainable.* | CSM logs detailed product feedback with customer context. Sets honest expectations on roadmap — do not over-promise. Explore workarounds. Resolved when feature ships OR customer accepts a workaround and the friction is removed. | **CSM + Product (feedback channel)** | Anna if feature gap is driving churn risk |
| **Support Tickets** | Customer has a high volume of open, unresolved, or recurring support tickets indicating systemic issues or dissatisfaction. | *Multiple open tickets. Tickets open > 5 business days without resolution. Customer expresses frustration about support responsiveness.* | CSM monitors ticket queue and escalates stale tickets. Identify if there is a pattern (bug, training gap, integration issue) and address the root cause — not just the tickets. Resolved when tickets are closed and customer confirms satisfaction. | **CSM + Support** | Anna if pattern suggests systemic issue |
| **Lost Champion** | The customer's primary internal advocate — the person who championed [Company] internally and drove adoption — has left the company or changed roles. | *CSM learns champion has left in a call, via LinkedIn, or through another contact. EB Interaction Score drops. Engagement drops suddenly.* | CSM identifies the new champion immediately. Triggers New Stakeholder Onboarding playbook. Re-establishes the relationship with the new contact. Does not assume the next person will have the same buy-in. Resolved when new champion is identified, onboarded, and actively engaged. | **CSM** | Anna if no new champion can be identified within 2 weeks |
| **Leadership Turnover** | A senior leader or executive — CEO, COO, Director of Admissions, Director of BD — has changed at the customer organization. | *Customer mentions leadership change. LinkedIn update. New name appears in emails or calls.* | CSM triggers Leadership Turnover playbook. Sends Leadership Turnover Outreach email. Re-establishes the executive relationship with the new leader. Proactively introduces [Company] value before the new leader questions the investment. Resolved when new leader is engaged and aligned. | **CSM (+ Anna for executive-to-executive if needed)** | Anna if new leader is expressing skepticism about [Company] |
| **Leadership Alignment** | Existing leadership is not bought in, is disengaged, or is actively questioning the value of [Company]. The economic buyer is not aligned. | *EB Interaction Score is 0.01 (90+ days no contact). Customer mentions leadership concerns. CSM cannot get access to the decision maker.* | CSM escalates to Anna immediately — this typically requires an EBR or executive-to-executive conversation. CSM cannot resolve Leadership Alignment issues alone. Resolved when the executive sponsor is re-engaged and has confirmed continued support. | **CSM + Anna (executive-to-executive)** | Anna immediately — do not try to handle alone |
| **Customer Adoption** | Product usage is low or declining. Users are not logging in, features are not being used, or the platform is not embedded in daily workflows. | *Product Usage Score < 0.50. 1-day or 7-day usage ratio dropping. CSM observes low engagement in calls. Customer reports staff not using the system.* | CSM reviews all three product usage sub-scores to identify where the drop is. Schedules an enablement session or sends targeted Loom content. Identifies the specific blocker — is it training, a missing feature, a workflow issue, or leadership not enforcing usage? Resolved when usage trend reverses and is sustained. | **CSM (+ Tech Services if integration-related)** | Anna if adoption is not recovering after 30 days of intervention |
| **Data Migration Issue** | Customer data migration is incomplete, delayed, significantly inaccurate, or causing operational disruption. | *Customer reports missing data. Migration timeline has slipped. Data mapping errors discovered post-migration.* | CSM + Tech Services / IS team. Identify the specific data gap and root cause. Set a clear remediation timeline with the customer. Do not minimize the issue — data problems erode trust fast. Resolved when data is confirmed accurate and complete by the customer. | **CSM + IS + Tech Services** | Anna if migration issue is blocking go-live or causing significant customer distress |
| **Timeline Risk** | An implementation or project milestone is at risk of slipping significantly, putting go-live or a key deliverable at risk. | *Integration credentials not received. Configuration sessions rescheduled multiple times. Customer not responsive to scheduling. IS flags milestone at risk.* | CSM and IS escalate to Anna if the timeline is significantly at risk. Identify the blocker — is it on the customer side or [Company] side? Create a revised timeline with the customer and hold them to it. Resolved when timeline is back on track and confirmed by all parties. | **CSM + IS** | Anna if go-live date is at risk by more than 2 weeks |
| **Potential Churn Risk** | There is a broad signal — not tied to one specific issue — that the customer may not renew. This is the most serious flag type. | *Customer expresses dissatisfaction broadly. Multiple other flags are open simultaneously. Renewal conversation is stalling. Customer goes dark. CSM gut feel after a difficult call.* | File this flag AND trigger the At-Risk or Churn Save playbook immediately. Anna must be looped in immediately — this is not a solo mission. Document a Save Plan on the Renewal Opp in SFDC. Resolved only when the renewal is secured and signed. | **CSM + Anna (immediately)** | Anna immediately — no exceptions |
